Team SWELDD

In summary, the group felt that we have significantly improved since last time. We were proactive in the tasks that were assigned to us and everyone was present in most of the meetings we had along the way. The group collectively decided to go with the tried and tested tools that we used in Assignment two as we were comfortable using them effectively with the experience gained by using them in the previous assignment.

As mentioned in the individual statements above, as a group we decided to go with Google Docs and Discord for collaboration, communication and record keeping. As a result, our GitHub audit trail may not reflect the work that the group has put in and may reflect that we have not used GitHub effectively. This however is rectified by our chosen tools for collaborating instead of GitHub.

It is the view of some members that we have left some tasks to the last minute and that our task scheduling could have been better. We would be able to improve this by setting more hard and fast goals to be completed within set dates.

The highlight and most important task of this assignment was the project. The group came up with some great ideas in our regular meetings. We discussed these ideas and other task scheduling points at our meetings. After some deliberation we decided to go with ‘Kutulu, The Monster Helper’ that was suggested by one of our members as highlighted in the individual descriptions above. The group felt that it was current and relevant in today’s society because mental health is being more widely understood as a real issue among all generations. And we felt that not every individual would come forward to seek help or talk about it with someone or on an open forum. So, our solution was to get some advice anonymously and at any time when a person needs it, using the most sophisticated electronic communication tools that are now available to us all. We think the best way to achieve this is by introducing artificial intelligence that can communicate effectively about the subject, namely an interactive chatbot that is available at any given time on any device for immediate help.

In realising the enormity of this task, we realised that we will not be able to come up with a working model of an artificially intelligent bot within the few weeks available to us. We have however, made an effort to show how this can be achieved with two prototypes being completed, one in Java and the other in Javascript.

If we were going to take one thing away as a group, it would be to make sure the entire group was aware that their amount of contribution could affect their mark at the completion of our course. The contributions form was misunderstood by some, as a result some members did not contribute as much as others and this was highlighted in the form. This caused some difficult conversations to take place in the final stages of our assignment. By this stage it was too late and the outcome was that some members were dissatisfied in how much they contributed along the way.

In closing, we learnt a massive amount while conducting this course and while learning to work in groups. We all enjoyed each of the team members input and formed some great bonds along the way.

This was a very positive experience.

We hope you enjoyed our project as much as we enjoyed creating it.

Sweldd.
