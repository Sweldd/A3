Group Processes

The group worked together well in Assignment 2. There were some good and bad points as we were just starting to learn how to work in teams.

Some of the good things the group undertook were, the use of collaboration tools (such as Discord, google docs and GitHub), kept a timeline document, tracked all meetings with a meeting agenda and made our intentions clear with a group charter.

Some of the not so good experiences were, poor team meeting organisation, poor communication and understanding of the assignment specifications, typical communication errors within the group using voice chat, delayed initial planning of the assignment and issues not meeting the timeline goals set by the group.

All of these issues have been addressed for the remainder of the course with our group. Our team set to work immediately and had tried and tested foundations to work with. We discussed the areas that needed improvement prior to commencing and all members agreed to improving them. The group has really improved as a result and we are finding the assignment was conducted in a more efficient manner.

All team members were happy with the communication on Discord and using google docs in canvas. This was after a relatively slow start where we did not communicate as effectively as we could have from the beginning. There was definitely room for improvement! We scored average marks for assignment 2 and the team are determined this time to make amends and pull off a solid mark. We will be changing some of our techniques (ie inserting a table of contents into our report and capturing all of our artefacts) and processes (Ensuring we go through our report prior to final submission and productively scrutinise and identify any areas we may be lacking in). We also identified that our GitHub usage was not as detailed as what it should have been. This time around we plan on either using GitHib or elaborating on what we did use instead and why.

We have already started off in a better way! Our communication from the start of assignment 3 was very good. We have tracked every meeting with written agendas, created an artefacts page to capture all of the interesting things we have investigated or used along the way, utilised another group charter as a sign of good faith to the team and created another timeline that we plan on being a massive help in our planning and goal setting throughout the assignment.

Career Plans


Our groups ideal Jobs

  • Jordan - Business Analyst
  • Francis - Systems Security Analyst
  • Suki - Research Scientist in Machine Learning
  • Sophie - Data Analyst
  • Chris - Project Developer

As the IT Industry is so varied, it certainly makes sense that each member in our group has chosen a different ideal job to discuss. Members of our group have chosen jobs in the field of analysts, researchers and developers.

Three members of the group have chosen analyst jobs including Business Analyst, Systems Security Analyst and Data Analyst. Even though these three jobs share part of the same name and are positions commonly found in businesses, they are vastly different and require uniquely different skill sets. For example a business analyst would typically work in a business management team and help make important decisions about business IT operations and software, while a data analyst may share the same office, they would be involved in projects where large amounts of data are being collected, processed and implemented into different projects. The third analyst job, systems security analyst, would require yet another unique skill set to be successful.

People involved in industries such as machine learning and green energy certainly have the power to change the world and make it a better place for everyone. It can be easily seen that jobs in the clean energy sector are likely to increase as society progresses to be more environmentally friendly. If this happens, many more jobs will become available including project developer and management jobs. As the clean energy sector if often involved with cutting edge technology, it makes perfect sense to have a good understanding of IT or even better, hold a degree. Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence seems to be dominating the cutting edge of Information Technology at the moment. This is no surprise as the potential for this technology is enormous! But with this technology comes great responsibility and the need for knowledge and regulation at an early stage to prevent any catastrophe. This is why researchers and scientists in this field will have such an important job now and in the future.

It can clearly be seen that the IT industry will continue to dominate now and in the future in terms of business and improving the world that we live in.
